Integrating with AllStar® Connect#

From a technical, app developer point of view having an SDK often makes the integration work easier and faster. From a device manufacturer point of view though it is very cumbersome to maintain and LCM an SDK given the medical device regulatory requirements. This is due to the complexity of managing deployment, release, and updates across many smart device systems across many 3rd party vendors. (Example: New phones and mobile OS versions would require additional Verification and coordination activities between legal entities even though the device itself including its Firmware has not changed.)

The model implemented incurs more up front effort, but enables more efficient and less complex Life Cycle Management and Post Market Surveillance activities.

It was a conscious decision to NOT provide an SDK together with the device. We therefore focus on a well described instructions manual together with sample code (in this document) and a Hardware tool in form of a pen device simulator (see here) to support 3rd party app developers.

Overview of assets intended to be supplied:

Device/OS compatibility list#

The CONNECTED DEVICE supports data communication using Bluetooth Low Energy, based upon specification version 4.2 (see here).

For iOS integration, the minimum OS version this could support is iOS 8. For ease of development and maintenance, we recommend no less than iOS 11 as the supported minimum (2 less than current major version as of Jan 2020). (The iPhone 6 or newer support Bluetooth 4.2.)

For Android integration, is required that the device supports Bluetooth 4.2. For ease of development and maintenance, we recommend no less than Android 8 as the supported minimum (2 less than current major version as of Jan 2020).

Use-Related Risks#

The listed risks are those that are not fully mitigated by the device alone. These system risks must be further mitigated as part of the app design development process.

The risk controls below are inputs to the risk and development process of the app. They must be contextualized and incorporated into the app design (which is unknown and will vary by vendors). This means the app risks and controls traced to the below risk controls will be based on the actual app design.

Flag Handling

The pen provides information on the speed of an injection. In theory, it flags events that are likely airshots, that is, no insulin was expelled during the injection. This can result from lack of priming of the piston rod or users who forget to insert a cartridge altogether. This information needs to be communicated adequately in order to prevent users from overly relying on this flag. The flag does not work for units smaller than FAST_DOSE_UNITS. So airshots smaller than FAST_DOSE_UNITS remain undetected by the system. The user needs to be aware of this.

End of Life#

The AllStar® Connect Device has been designed to be used for 1-3 years, but at least 1 year after first use. The useful life of the AllStar® Connect depends on the usage of the device and ambient temperature.

The Device shall NOT be used any longer once the battery is empty or the storage is full even though it is mechanically working for a maximum of 3 years.

It is desired that an estimated remaining use time (derived from the transmitted values) is provided to the user via the App.

As the estimation of the remaining in use lifetime of the pen device based on a battery capacity prediction is currently lacking sufficient data, alternative approaches to send out warnings via an app should be considered:

Battery status#

As the device gets used over time the battery voltage will decrease. Once this value has dropped below LOW_VOLTAGE the Low Voltage Flag will be set and should be leveraged to indicate a warning that the pen device has to be replaced in near future.

Parameters:

  • Low Voltage Flag: provided via the Dose Record (see here)
  • With ongoing use lower thresholds of VERY_LOW_VOLTAGE and BROWN_OUT_VOLTAGE (see here) will raise error codes 96 or 104 (see here)
  • Battery Voltage value: provided via the Extended Dose Record (see here)

Info

AllStar® Connect is powered by a 3 V CR1225 Lithium Coin Cell which is not rechargeable or replaceable.

A measurement of a very low battery voltage may indicate the coin cell is close to end of life but may also be due to a low temperature (from, say, storing in a refrigerator) depressing the voltage, which may recover when the temperature of the device increases.

Dose record capacity#

The Dose Record Capacity is limited by the RECORD_STORAGE_SIZE. The remaining free memory can be calculated with following inputs:

Parameters:

  • RECORD_STORAGE_SIZE
  • Record Number of the latest dose record provided via the Extended Dose Record (see here)

Based on the amount of dose records collected in the last month, a prediction can be made how long the remaining memory will last. This prediction should be provided as a time frame to the patient via the App.

  • Free Memory / SUM(Dose Records (last 4 weeks))

Info

AllStar® Connect is able to store up to RECORD_STORAGE_SIZE dose records. Once the memory is full, the device is NOT able to generate new records. While the memory is full, AllStar® Connect transmits the final record over and over again, or provides older records if requested. At this point also the Auto-Sync is deactivated.

Time restrictions#

The maximum useful life of the pen device is estimated to be 3 years for all mechanical functions. Based on the information retrieved in the Extended Dose Record concerning the First Use Time and the time when the last dose was applied (Dose Time), a calculation can be performed to determine if the remaining useful time is less than 3 months. Alternatively, the time provided by the mobile device to the App can be used. In this case, the App should indicate a warning and display the remaining days.

Parameters:

  • First Use Time: provided via the Extended Dose Record (see here)
  • Dose Time: provided via the Dose Record (see here)

Another warning should be raised if the time between the Production date and First Use Time is greater than 2 years – the warning should highlight that the maximum storage time has been exceeded.

Parameters:

  • First Use Time: provided via the Extended Dose Record (see here)
  • Production date as part of the UDI (see here)

A warning should be issued when the Dose Time value indicates the Time Error 0x1FFFF8 after approximately 3.99 years in use, as the internal counter has exceeded the boundary and is in undefined state.

Parameters:

  • Dose Time value = error code 0x1FFFF8: (see here)

A device expiration date is currently not supported by the API.

Complaint handling#

In order to support Sanofi´s complaint (PTC) handling processes of the AllStar® Connect the App Developer must implement means to provide data for such purpose.

PTC investigation#

It is required to have appropriate means to allow for pulling of specific device data for PTC investigations. (For example provision of log data by device serial number query.) This includes gathering and transmitting of relevant data such as SN/LOT number, battery voltage, CPU temperature, error logs and/or device use data (wear level indicator).

Respective information is provided via the Extended Dose Record (see here)

The process and responsibility details will manifest in agreements.

PTC trending data#

This includes gathering and transmitting of relevant data such as SN/LOT number, battery voltage, CPU temperature, error logs and/or device use data (wear level indicator), but must comply with data privacy regulations and must not contain patient data.
